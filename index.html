      What an Art Description Should Include

      1 - Your Inquiry (Big Idea):

      • What question are you exploring?
      • What theme connects your work?

      Example:

      "This piece explores how cultural identity shifts between private and public spaces."

      2 - Intent (Why You Made It)

      • What were you trying to communicate?
      • What meaning does the work have?

      Example:

      "I wanted to show the contrast between internal anxiety and external confidence."

      3 - Materials & Process

      • Medium (acrylic, charcoal, digital photography, mixed media, etc.)
      • Techniques used
      • Any experimentation or revision

      Example:

      "I used layered acrylic glazes and palette knife textures to create depth and emotional intensity."

      4 - Art Elements & Principles

      • Mention how you used the Elements of Art and Principles of Design

      Example:

      "The asymmetrical composition and high contrast lighting emphasize isolation."

      Complete Example:

      "In this piece, I explore how cultural identity shifts between private and public spaces by contrasting internal desire with the restrictive Superego. I used layered acrylic glazes and palette knife textures to build depth and emotional tension. The asymmetrical composition and high-contrast lighting reinforce a sense of isolation."
